⚡ Quick Start — 4 steps
1
Import — tap + Import or the camera button to load your image
2
Size — set your engraving width and height in mm to match your material
3
Material — pick your material from the dropdown. The app sets everything else automatically
4
Export PNG — tap the red button at the bottom. Done

That is all you need. The sliders and advanced settings are optional fine-tuning — you do not need to touch them for great results.

📋 What the preset buttons do
⚡ Simple Guided step-by-step mode for beginners. Walks you through size, material and export in a clean popup.
Auto Prep Applies a balanced general-purpose preset. Good starting point for most images on most materials.
Smart MDF Analyses your image scientifically and applies optimised settings specifically for MDF. Uses histogram analysis to pick the best dithering pattern automatically.
🚧 Basswood Preset tuned for pale basswood ply. Lifts shadows and boosts local contrast to compensate for basswood's low burn contrast.
Perfect Engrave Reads the processed image, analyses its tonal range and contrast, then automatically picks the best dithering algorithm for that specific image.
HQ Mode Doubles the DPI for finer detail. Slower to process and produces a larger file. Best for small detailed engravings.
⚙ How the processing works

Every image goes through a scientifically correct pipeline:

Resize Mitchell-Netravali bicubic resampling — the same algorithm used by professional print software. Preserves fine detail when scaling down.
Greyscale Rec.709 luminance formula (the broadcast TV standard) — weights red, green and blue correctly for human perception rather than averaging them equally.
Tonal Contrast, brightness, gamma and black/white point are applied in the correct scientific order to preserve detail across the full tonal range.
Dithering Converts greyscale to pure black and white using error-diffusion or ordered patterns. This is what makes gradients look smooth when engraved. The material preset picks the right algorithm automatically.
DPI The exported PNG has DPI metadata embedded so your laser controller knows the exact physical size without you needing to enter it manually.
⏱ Processing time

Most images process in 5–15 seconds. Large images at high DPI or with error-diffusion dithering (Floyd-Steinberg, Jarvis, Stucki) can take up to 60 seconds. The app is working — the spinning circle and progress bar confirm this. Do not close the app while processing.

💡 Tips

Change one thing at a time. The image re-processes every time you adjust a slider — if you move several at once you will not know which one made the difference.

The A/B button in the preview lets you compare the original and processed versions side by side by dragging the divider.

Double-tap any slider to reset it to its default value.

Your settings are automatically saved and restored next time you open the app.
